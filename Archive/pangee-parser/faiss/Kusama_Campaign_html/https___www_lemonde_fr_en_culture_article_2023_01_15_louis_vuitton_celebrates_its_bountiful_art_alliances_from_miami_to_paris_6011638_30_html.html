Louis Vuitton celebrates its bountiful art alliances from Miami to Paris

Between its large-scale collaboration with Yayoi Kusama and its new exhibition venue in Paris, the world's number one luxury goods company continues to strengthen its relationships with visual artists.

By Roxana Azimi

Published on January 15, 2023, at 10:30 am (Paris)

6 min read

Lire en franais
  • Share on Facebook
  • Share by email
  • Share on Linkedin

Subscribers only

From December 1 to December 3, visitors at Art Basel Miami came face to face with two life-size, Madame Tussauds-like wax sculptures of Japanese artist Yayoi Kusama. One was wearing a red dress studded with white polka dots, the other a white dress covered with multicolored dots. Both were wearing the famous red wig inseparably linked to the image of the 93-year-old artist. Mentally ill, she has been voluntarily living in a five-star Japanese psychiatric institute since 1977, where she still creates her trademark works of art full of polka dots.

The two statues were there to welcome visitors to the stand of the French luxury goods company Louis Vuitton, set up alongside those of art galleries from around the world. Owned by the LVMH group, the iconic fashion brand was already present last October at the first edition of Paris+ at the Grand Palais Ephmre, where it exhibited but did not sell a few select archival pieces for the very first time. Yet its Florida stand was a new type of project. It marked the unveiling of a large-scale collaboration between Louis Vuitton and Ms. Kusama, who applied her colorful dots to the brand's men's and women's products: silk twill pajamas, leather skirts, cashmere sweaters, wool coats, eyewear, bobs, sneakers, loafers... They will be available for purchase in January 2023. Rarely before had a space of this scale been given to an artist within a luxury house.
