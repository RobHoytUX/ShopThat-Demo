Yayoi Kusama and the Power of Louis Vuitton

What happens when two creative worlds meet each other? Louis Vuitton invited world-famous Japanese artist Yayoi Kusama to join for a project - and even developed a "Yayoi robot".

18.01.2023 by Lisa Ditzlm√ºller

The liaison between fashion and art has always existed. The influence of artists and photographers is highly valued by fashion designers worldwide. What these creative minds have in common is their creative ability to express emotions and impulses.

A sculpture by Yayoi Kusama at the Louis Vuitton store on the Avenue des Champs-√âlys√©es in Paris is attracting a lot of attention.

A unique campaign: Louis Vuitton x Yayoi Kusama

An artist who is a source of fascination in many places and is known for her collaborations with Louis Vuitton is Yayoi Kusama. For the current PR campaign, the fashion house is covered in dots most spectacularly. On the Avenue des Champs-√âlys√©es in Paris, an oversized sculpture by the artist towers over the luxury brand's store. It looks like she is adding another dot to the fa√ßade of the Louis Vuitton shop, which is already covered in countless colourful dots.

In a store on 5th Avenue in New York, a life-like robot that looks amazingly like Yayoi Kusama has been placed in the shop window. There, it suggests drawing dots on the shop window and can even move its eyes, head and the corners of its mouth to look at and frighten perplexed passers-by. At the Tokyo shop, a screen has been installed over an entire floor, showing a fascinating 3D animation. A Louis Vuitton box opens and three pumpkins appear. Based on Kusama's iconic bob hairstyle, the artist's head is behind one pumpkin, peering curiously down over the edge of the screen at the pedestrians.

The artist and Louis Vuitton's advertising posters and campaign installations are admired all over the world and promptly went viral on social media. Louis Vuitton provided a matching filter with which you can conjure up the colourful dots on your face and an app with games and opportunities to win.

Japanese artist Yayoi Kusama is one of the most successful artists in the world. (Photo credit: Instagram @yayoikusamamuseum)

The dark story behind the dots

But what is actually behind all the dots? Yayoi Kusama has suffered from obsessive-compulsive disorder and hallucinations since the age of 12. She was born in Matsumoto (Japan) in 1929 and grew up traditionally conservative under strict educational measures. At the same age, she had to work in a parachute factory as a contribution to the Second World War at her parent's insistence.

Already during her childhood, she discovered her passion for painting. She went to her grandfather's field every day to draw the flowers there. The day her hallucinations began, she was in the field again. This time she heard the flowers speaking to her. Her hallucinations became more and more severe, she developed an obsessive-compulsive disorder. Kusama‚Äôs parents had not much understanding of their child's illness.

Art helped her process her visualisations and eased her fears. Painting dots makes her feel safe. Because she is only protected from what lies under a network of dots. This is how her current trademark came into being - the polka dots - which will accompany her throughout her life. Dots mean love and peace to her. They are a symbol of infinity and the dissolution of boundaries in their endless, unknowable form. Yayoi Kusama has been undergoing psychiatric treatment since 1952. 

Polka dots can‚Äôt stay alone. When we obliterate nature and our bodies with polka dots we become part of the unity of our environments. - Yayoi Kusama

Three fragrances from the current collaboration

The fashion house appreciates the artist and her work. Before the start of the big campaign, Kusama last met Louis Vuitton in 2012. During their current collaboration, three extraordinary fragrances were created, drawn with Kusama's motifs.

Attrape-R√™ves smells of a peony bouquet fused with raw cocoa powder, a little patchouli and gets a fresh note from lychee. L'Immensit√© is the men's fragrance in the collection. The men's fragrance is decorated with Painted Dots in black, silver, grey and white. 

Yayoi Kusama developed her trademark Infinity Dots from her Painted Dots. The motif consists of a repeating dot pattern and was used for the fragrance Spell On You. It carries a sensual fragrance note combined of iris and rose. The dots also decorate the leather travel case.

The LV X YK KEEPALL 45 travel bag with faithful reproduction of the colourful, hand-painted PAINTED DOTS and impressive 3D effect.

Art on Fashion

As early as May 2022, the Louis Vuitton Cruise 2023 show in San Diego offered the first glimpses of the many designs created in collaboration with the artist. The ready-to-wear collection includes fragrances, shoes, accessories, bags, luggage and suitcases. Also here, the Painted Dots are the defining motif. They were incorporated through a complex screen-printing technique and embossing on leather or iconic coated canvases.  The screen printing technique reproduces Kusama's brush strokes and creates a 3D effect on the fashion pieces.

The metal dots used in selected pieces are intended to express the infinite. Inspired by Yayoi Kusama's mirrored spheres from her daffodil garden, they reflect the light and create a stunning effect. Also included in the collection are Infinity Dots and Psychedelic Flowers. Flowers are fascinating for Yayoi Kusama. They show the fleeting beauty of the ephemeral and yet are associated with eternity and infinity.

The creator's signature motifs seduce us into her world and show boundlessness and creativity in a unique way. The collection is a true masterpiece of craftsmanship that offers beautiful fashion pieces for women and men alike.

Moschino's Chinese New Year Capsule collection features 23 unisex fashion pieces with the iconic bunny "Bugs Bunny". (Photo credit: Instagram @moschino)

Moschino's artistic vein

Artistic fashion styles are also very popular at Moschino. To celebrate the "Year of the Rabbit", the fashion house launches a new collection featuring the iconic cartoon rabbit Bugs Bunny. With the Chinese New Year Capsule collection, Moschino collaborates with Warner Bros. and presents 23 unisex pieces with Bugs Bunny motifs under the direction of creative director Jeremy Scott. From cardigans to skirts, accessories and shoes, the entire collection revolves around Bugs Bunny. Even the Biker Bag, the classic of the house, was given bunny ears. 

Jeremy Scott must be particularly fond of the bunny: Back in 2015, he created his own collection with the characters from the Looney Tunes animated film series by Warner Bros. Alongside Sylvester, Tweety and Co., the cheeky bunny grinned from the catwalk at the Autumn-Winter 2015 Ready-to-Wear Fashion Show.
