LOUIS VUITTON AND YAYOI KUSAMA

TAKE OVER NEW YORK CITY

THE HIGHLY-ANTICIPATED COLLABORATION UNVEILS IN A SERIES OF EXPERIENTIAL POP-UP BOUTIQUES.

BY VIENNA VERNOSE JANUARY 6, 2023

A decade following their inaugural collaboration, Louis Vuitton and Yayoi Kusama unravel their highly-anticipated collaboration across the globe in the form of immersive pop-up shops. Initially debuting in Tokyo, the French Maison and iconic Japanese artists bring their creative vision to life at two respective physical retail spaces in New York City’s Meatpacking and SoHo districts.

MEATPACKING DISTRICT

Courtesy of Louis Vuitton

Inside the dual brick-and-mortar storefronts, Louis Vuitton and Kusama’s world takes shape in spaces that invite guests to intimately explore the collection. Upon entering through the Meatpacking District pop-up’s glass façade, Kusama’s use of repetition floods the store in the form of hypnotic chrome spheres and polka dot patterns. At the SoHo space, rainbow polka dot splotches coat the walls, ceiling, and floor of the space as if walking into one of Kusama’s pieces. Its experiential qualities mimic that of Kusama’s museum exhibitions, the only difference is, the entire scope of the coveted collection is available for purchase. 

LOUIS VUITTON X YAYOI KUSAMA

SOHO

Courtesy of Louis Vuitton

Spanning across accessories, fragrance, luggage, menswear, handbags, and several other categories, Kusama has placed her artistic flair on practically every corner of Louis Vuitton’s sartorial repertoire. Testing the limits of the Maison’s savor-fair, fine details shine through in adapting Kusama’s work for the first release of the multi-drop collection. Kusama’s signature polka dots find their way onto Louis Vuitton’s heritage pieces, like a classic Speedy 25 reimagined in red and white polka dots and an eye-catching black Cannes handbag accentuated with Kusama’s chrome spheres.

At the Maison’s flagship boutique on Fifth Avenue and 57th Street in New York City, passersby have congregated to the boutique’s display to admire a hyper-realistic animatronic of Kusama painting her polka dots on the window glass. With unique facial expressions, hand gestures, and following eyes, social media users questioned if the animatronic was possibly the real Kusama.

While the Louis Vuitton X Kusama collection has generated lots of hype over the last several months, the Maison recently reveled the collaboration’s accompanying campaign sending the fashion wold into frenzy. Starring runway icons like Gisele Bündchen, Devon Aoki, Anok Yai, Christy Turlington, Bella Hadid, and many more, the campaign brought together fashion’s forces with an equally powerful creative team consisting of photography by Steven Meisel and styling by CR‘s EIC Carine Roitfeld. Expanding on Louis Vuitton’s dialog with the world of fine art, the collaboration culminates in a a 360-degree universe inviting guests into the world of Louis Vuitton X Yayoi Kusama.

The Louis Vuitton X Yayoi Kusama collection is now available to show at Louis Vuitton boutiques at on louisvuitton.com.
