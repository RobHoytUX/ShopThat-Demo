A giant flower by Takashi Murakami rises from the ground in Paris

The Japanese artist has just left his mark on the capital with a beautiful work in the heart of the Jardin d'Acclimatation.

Alexandre Lasry - Journalist • December 16, 2024

SHARE THE ARTICLE

If you’re familiar with Takashi Murakami‘s colorful, joyful world, you’ll have no trouble recognizing his latest work in Paris. Since last weekend, two golden flowers, one of them giant, have taken up residence on a lawn in the Jardin d’Acclimatation. In collaboration with Louis Vuitton, the Japanese artist unveiled “The Flower parent and the child”, depicting his famous characters on a Louis Vuitton trunk, to mark the revival of their joint collection dating back to 2003. Discover it now!

Takashi Murakami unveils a monumental work in Paris

Following the successful conclusion of his exhibition at Galerie Perrotin Matignon, Takashi Murakami continues to make Paris his playground. As proof, one of his works can now be found outdoors, in the Bois de Boulogne and the Jardin d’Acclimatation. Decorated in gold, this work represents a parent and child, in the form of the artist’s emblematic smiling flowers.

The structure stands on a white Louis Vuitton trunk, adorned with multicolored logos. For those of you who keep up to date with fashion news, you may have recognized the very first capsule collection between the artist and the brand, back in 2003. A colorful twist on the world of the French fashion house, this capsule created quite a stir, and will be revisited from January 2025.

This is not the first time Louis Vuitton has collaborated with an artist from the land of the rising sun. In 2023, the French brand erected a giant statue of Japanese designer Yayoi Kusama in front of its headquarters, right next to the Samaritaine, not without reaction from many Parisians.
