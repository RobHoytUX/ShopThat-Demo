Cherry Blossom Season: LV x Murakami Drops This Week

By
Carrine Yem
-
March 12, 2025

Louis Vuitton continues to celebrate its iconic partnership with Japanese artist Takashi Murakami, marking 20 years since their groundbreaking collaboration. This re-edition collection is set to be pre-launched online on March 14 and available globally in stores from March 21.

Originally launched in 2003 under Marc Jacobs’ creative direction, the duo redefined the LV Monogram with bold reinterpretations, including the rainbow-hued pattern and cherry blossom motifs. Following the 2003 launch, the collaboration became one of the most influential collaborations in the industry. 

The second chapter of their Spring 2025 collection focuses on Murakami’s Cherry Blossom motif, celebrating Japan’s poetic cherry blossom season. Featuring over 40 pieces, the collection pays homage to the beauty of nature while blending traditional and modern artistic styles.

The new chapter builds on the success of the first instalment launched earlier this year, which featured over 200 pieces inspired by Murakami’s original 2003 designs. Zendaya returns as the face of the campaign, photographed in dreamy urban settings surrounded by floating cherry blossoms.

Highlights from the collection include a range of city bags, silk Carrés, sneakers, and sandals adorned with the Cherry Blossom motif. Larger items such as rolling trunks and made-to-order Malle Wardrobes also showcase the design, alongside smaller leather goods like the Nano Bucket bag and Zippy wallet.

Other standout pieces include the Pochette Accessoires Sakura Brown, Speedy 25 Sakura Brown, and Capucines Mini & BB Rose. Additionally, Murakami’s blossoms grace a reimagined version of Louis Vuitton’s Spell on You perfume bottle, packaging, and travel case.
